Write the course objectives

Gemini Pro
▸ Prompt
Context We are building an undergraduate elective, "Introduction to Food Safety", for second-year students at an Indian agricultural university — mixed backgrounds (agriculture, food technology, dairy, veterinary). Use how Cornell, UC Davis, Wageningen and Michigan State frame an intro food-safety course, and the UGC Learning-Outcomes-based Curriculum Framework (LOCF): course outcomes with Bloom's-level verbs. Role You are an instructional designer. Task Write 6–8 course objectives; under each, 1–2 measurable outcomes that are assessable in this course. Cover biological / chemical / physical hazards, hygiene & HACCP, and the Indian regulatory context. State the prior knowledge you are assuming. Format Numbered objectives, indented outcomes with the Bloom's level in brackets. One page. List the universities and the framework you drew on at the end. Do not invent citations — if unsure a source is real, say so.
▸ Output
Gemini Pro · Introduction to Food Safety (2 credits, 2nd year). Prior knowledge assumed: first-year biology and chemistry; no prior food science required.

Course objectives & learning outcomes

  • 1. Appreciate the scale and significance of food safety. Describe the global and Indian burden of foodborne illness and identify who is most at risk. [Understand]
  • 2. Distinguish the types of food hazard. Classify a given food risk as biological, chemical or physical, with an example. [Analyse] Explain the difference between a hazard and a risk. [Understand]
  • 3. Trace where contamination enters the food chain. Map the farm-to-fork chain for a chosen food and locate the main hazard points. [Apply]
  • 4. Apply hygiene and HACCP principles to a simple process. State the seven HACCP principles and identify a critical control point in a simple process. [Apply]
  • 5. Describe the regulatory framework for food safety. Explain the roles of Codex, FAO/WHO and FSSAI, and the basic obligations of a food business in India. [Understand]
  • 6. Evaluate a real food operation. Assess a real food operation and propose realistic, low-cost control measures. [Evaluate] Judge food-safety evidence critically, distinguishing a supported claim from an unsupported one. [Evaluate]

Benchmarks drawn on

Introductory food-safety and food-science courses at Cornell, UC Davis, Wageningen University & Research, and Michigan State; the UGC Learning-Outcomes-based Curriculum Framework (LOCF) for outcome structure and Bloom's verbs.

Model's own caveat: the university course framings above are named from general knowledge — confirm the specific syllabi before citing them. This is exactly the kind of claim we will verify in NotebookLM.
