Case study — inventing a course with AI

Gemini Pro + NotebookLM
G.B. Pant University of
Agriculture & Technology
The example: invent “Introduction to Food Safety” — a one-month, 12-lecture undergraduate elective, built from real sources.
Chosen because every discipline relates to it and it is rich in open courseware and papers. The method is identical for any topic — swap it freely.
Gemini Pro gathers and drafts fast — but ungrounded, it can invent a fact or a citation. NotebookLM grounds everything in your own sources and cites every claim. Used together, scattered readings become a course you can trust.
Create & gatherGemini Pro
1

Write the course objectives

Frame learning outcomes against UGC norms and how leading universities teach the topic.

→ objectives & outcomes
2

Summarise the research

Pull key reviews and papers; capture the current state of the field in brief.

→ research brief
3

Draft the course outline

Shape the one-month course into coherent modules and themes.

→ module outline
4

Gather the sources

Find open courseware and syllabi from top universities; collect the actual papers and readings.

→ source pack
5

Build the calendar

Three lectures a week, twelve in the month — each lecture with its own objective.

→ 12-lecture calendar
6

Model the students

Generate synthetic student profiles — background, prior knowledge, goals — for the real room.

→ learner personas
7

Map each session to the learner

For all twelve sessions, map the key points from the student’s lens; attach papers, blogs and links per session.

→ per-session map
8

Draft the content

Full, verbose lecture drafts, session by session, for all twelve.

→ draft lectures
Ground & produceNotebookLM
9

Consolidate and ground

Load every source, paper, blog and draft into one notebook. Generate study guides, summaries, quizzes and an audio overview — each citing your own sources. Spot-check a few facts against their citations before you trust them.

→ grounded study guides, summaries and quizzes · minimal hallucination
Gemini creates; NotebookLM grounds. Every claim traces back to a source — that is what makes the material safe to hand a student.
Responsible use — upload only sources you are allowed to, verify before you use, disclose AI help, and remember the output goes out under your name.
