Video & graphical abstracts — safely, with Gemini

Use Gemini to make your research travel — a video abstract that plays at a conference, a graphical abstract that lands a paper. The one condition: your data and IP never enter the chat.
The rule that runs through today: placeholder in, real values out. Gemini writes and structures; you record, build, and keep your data. Raw datasets, unpublished results and proprietary methods stay out of the chat entirely — you substitute your real, sensitive values yourself, outside the conversation.
1

Video abstracts for conferences and papers

Gemini

Let Gemini write the script/narration and the storyboard; you do the recording and the visuals yourself. Gemini only ever sees a generic description — never raw datasets, unpublished results, or proprietary methods.

  1. Say the finding in one plain line — the message, with no sensitive numbers.
  2. Give Gemini a generic summary (aim, method in general terms, the headline result in words or a placeholder) and ask for a 60–90 second narration script.
  3. Ask for a scene-by-scene storyboard — shot, on-screen text, narration line, seconds.
  4. Refine length and tone iteratively (tighter, warmer, simpler).
  5. You: record the narration and build the visuals yourself — your slides, your figures, your voice. Gemini never touches the real assets.
  6. Optional: ask Gemini for a title, a caption and keywords for the upload.
▸ Prompt — Context · Role · Task · Format
Context I have a paper on [general topic]. Generic summary, no sensitive data: aim — [plain words]; method — [general approach]; headline finding — [plain words or a placeholder number]. I need a video abstract of about 75 seconds for a conference. Role You are a science communicator who writes tight video-abstract scripts. Task Write a ~75-second narration (about 140 words) in plain language, then a scene-by-scene storyboard. Format Script first; then a storyboard table (shot · on-screen text · narration · seconds). Do not ask me for raw data — work only from the generic summary.
Keep out: the raw dataset, unpublished results, and any proprietary method. The script works from your message, not your data.
2

Protecting your data and IP

The rule

This is what makes tasks 1 and 3 safe. A public AI chat is not a private vault — so share only what is already public or safely generic, and keep everything sensitive out.

Safe to share

  • The research question, in general terms
  • Already-published findings (cite them)
  • A generic description of the method — the approach, not the secret recipe
  • Placeholder / dummy numbers
  • The format or structure you want
  • Public background and context

Keep out entirely

  • Raw datasets
  • Unpublished results and figures
  • Exact values that are not yet public
  • Novel or proprietary methodology
  • Anything under embargo or NDA
  • Identifiable participant data
  1. Ask Session 1's test first: is this public, or mine to protect?
  2. Replace every sensitive value or method detail with a placeholder — [X], "Method A", "Site 1".
  3. Prompt with the placeholder version only.
  4. You: substitute your real values yourself, offline, in your own document.
  5. If a task genuinely needs the real data to run, use a private or institutional tool, not a public chat.
3

Designing graphical abstracts

Gemini

Ask Gemini for the layout logic, visual flow and iconography — using dummy / placeholder labels — then build the real graphic and drop in your sensitive values yourself, outside the conversation.

  1. Describe the story generically: problem → approach → result, with dummy labels.
  2. Ask for layout logic and visual flow — how the panels should read left to right at a glance.
  3. Ask for iconography and metaphor suggestions for each concept (still dummy labels).
  4. Ask for a simple colour and hierarchy scheme.
  5. You: build the graphic in your own tool (PowerPoint, Inkscape, BioRender, Canva) and substitute your real values and figures.
  6. Iterate the layout with Gemini using placeholders only — never the real numbers.
▸ Prompt — Context · Role · Task · Format
Context I am designing a graphical abstract for a paper on [general topic]. The story, with dummy labels: problem — [generic]; approach — [generic]; result — [placeholder]. It must read left to right in one glance. Role You are a scientific illustration designer. Task Propose a three-panel layout (problem → approach → result): what each panel shows, the visual flow between them, and icon or metaphor ideas for each concept. Suggest a simple colour hierarchy. Format A panel-by-panel plan, plus the icon ideas and the colour note. Use my dummy labels; do not ask for the real data.
Keep out: the real figures, exact values and any unpublished visual. Gemini designs the frame; you place the sensitive content inside it yourself.

The safe-sharing checklist

  • Public or placeholder in; real values out — every time.
  • Gemini writes and structures; you record, build and verify.
  • Real figures, raw data and unpublished methods → private/institutional tools only.
  • Cite published findings; keep unpublished work out of the chat.
  • Disclose AI assistance per your institution's norms — the output goes out under your name.
