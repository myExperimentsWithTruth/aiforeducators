The workflow we ran
Objectives→ Research→ Outline→ Sources→ Calendar→ Students→ Session map→ Draft (one at a time)→ Ground in NotebookLM
1

Grounding is the idea

NotebookLM answers only from the sources you give it, and cites every claim. That is what removes the invented facts and citations Session 1 warned about.

2

Two tools, two jobs

Gemini Pro creates and gathers — fast, but ungrounded. NotebookLM grounds and produces. Gemini creates; NotebookLM grounds.

3

The prompt does the work

A shallow prompt gives a shallow answer. Build every prompt as Context · Role · Task · Format. The difference is not the model — it is the prompt.

4

Decompose to keep quality

Draft one 60-minute lecture at a time, not twelve in one shot. One shot spreads the model thin and quality drops. Decomposition is the quality control.

5

Always verify a citation

Every study guide, quiz and answer cites a source. Spot-check one citation against the source before you trust the output.

6

Grounded ≠ finished

It only knows the sources you loaded. Mind the source limits, keep the human in the loop, and it still needs your judgement.

From scattered readings to a cited course — study guides, summaries and quizzes your students can trust, every claim traceable to a source.
Responsible use — upload only sources you are allowed to, disclose AI help, and remember the output goes out under your name.
